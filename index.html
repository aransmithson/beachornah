🏖️ How it works
📍 Location

Your browser location is used to find the nearest Environment Agency tide gauge and pull local weather. Your coordinates are saved locally so the app loads faster next time — hit ↻ to update them.

🌊 Tide height

The large number shows the current water level in metres from the nearest tidal gauge. The top-down map shows where the tide line sits between low and high water, and the arrow shows whether it's rising or falling based on the last few gauge readings.

☀️ Weather

Conditions, temperature, and wind speed come from Open-Meteo (free, no key needed). The beach scene animation reflects the real weather at your location — including rain, snow, fog, thunderstorms, and day/night.

🏄 The verdict

A simple thumbs up/down based on: below 12°C = chilly, wind over 30 mph = rough, rain or storms = stay home. You've been warned.

Data: EA Flood Monitoring API · Open-Meteo · Your device GPS
